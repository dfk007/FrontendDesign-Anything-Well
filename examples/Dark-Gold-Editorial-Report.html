How to Position Yourself in Applications

Your positioning statement

Don't say: "DevOps engineer with AI experience." Say: "AI Platform Infrastructure Engineer specialising in production multi-tenant LLM serving, agentic system deployment, and hybrid cloud AI infrastructure — with a track record building systems that sustain 99.9% uptime at token scale."

01Lead every application with ServeLLM as your proof point. It covers: multi-tenancy, RBAC, SSE, connection pooling, vector search, object storage, observability — a complete enterprise AI platform checklist.
02Frame your hybrid cloud (WireGuard + Ollama + GKE) as a cost architecture story: "I run sub-$10/day LLM inference by self-hosting on bare metal bridged to cloud auto-scaling." That's FinOps thinking, which most AI infra engineers lack.
03When asked about fine-tuning gaps: own it, then bridge it. "My strength is production serving infrastructure. I'm actively adding fine-tuning to my stack via QLoRA on local hardware — happy to walk through my current run."
04For agent roles: LangGraph is your entry. Emphasise state management, failure recovery, and observability — not just prompt chaining. That's the senior differentiator.
05For forward-deployed / startup roles: AgentApply + MyJobs shows you build AI-native products end-to-end, not just infrastructure. You can ship to users, not just to clusters.
06Cite the BSc from Goldsmiths for international applications — UK degree signals academic rigour to EU/US hiring managers who may not know Pakistani universities.
